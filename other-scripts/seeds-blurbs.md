\*\*The sieve poem (established spine):\*\*

How can I love  
someone  
when I arrive at a judgement  
at the first pulse?

I look past the skin, the flag,  
siphon the curation  
through my sieve, my eyes.

I carry my name like a tag,  
my name doesn't hold me.

\*\*Strongest standalone material:\*\*

You hold the dress up for me,  
a dress you'd never afford,  
and I'll buy but never wear.  
The dress shines lonely..

\*\*Sieve poem doughnuts (ranked):\*\*

A house with no books,  
or worse — with only one.

You flip burgers, like you flipped your dreams.

the heirloom diamond that endured nothing

carefully ripped jeans

torn blazer that saw decades

\*\*Other keepers:\*\*

Sleepy sheep sleeps / silently snoring solo.

we declare truce
