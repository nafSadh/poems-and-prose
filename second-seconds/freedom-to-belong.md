# Freedom to Belong

The blues bleed into reds, the whites flash into greens,  
and deep purples chase the orange glow  
as violet strobes shatter on the beat.

The crowd breathes, a single lung, a single heart.  
Feet don't just find the ground, they stomp it into dust.  
Hands don't just slice the air, they carve it into shapes,  
building a temple of temporary, chaotic grace.

Then the focus narrows to the space between two skins.  
The air, thick and heavy with desire,  
ignites a fire as bodies press close.  
Whispers of passion, a secret shared,  
and in the rhythm, all inhibitions are laid bare.

You surrender your bones to the bass,  
your thoughts to the heat, your name to the song.  
The world outside these walls disappears.  
There is only the music, only the movement.  
Only this.  
This freedom.
