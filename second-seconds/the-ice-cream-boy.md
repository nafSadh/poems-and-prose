# The Ice-cream Boy


Neon signs glitter a brittle freeze  
into the hollow ice cream shop.  
The curly haired boy walks in,  
settles at the corner.

A salty screech silences the clock,  
and a shy sparkle peeks out  
through the grumpy hoodie.

He stares through the glass  
with heavy breath, thirsty eyes,  
at twenty-six flavors,  
lost in a castle of frosty scoops.

He points.

Coins ring against the counter.  
A murmur that doesn't land anywhere.

He takes the booth.

The cold finds him before the sweet does.  
A smile cracks his stiff jaws.  
His eyes go wet.  
Beneath the freeze,  
something still knows how to melt.

Ice cream boy  
in the frosted kingdom,  
holding winter on his tongue.
